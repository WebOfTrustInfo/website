Suggested RWoT Paper Development Process

The suggested process below is one that the RWoT community has identified as resulting in the best papers. We define "best" as a respectful and collaborative effort between group participants often involving healthy debate and a balanced and thoughtful end product.

  1. During RWOT: topic ideation and development.
    • Team develops the topic and collaborate on the initial draft.
    • The topic group typically chooses a single discussion facilitator during the event (this person may be different from the "lead author" selected at the end of the event)
  2. Towards the end of RWoT, team discusses expectations for completing the paper. Specifically, team decides:
    • Lead author for post-event coordination
      1. The lead author is the person who will drive this process (for the group) after the event.
      2. The lead author is also empowered to make editorial decisions, but there are safety measures in place to allow for unresolvable conflicts.
    • Timeframe / schedule for the completion of the paper.
    • Contribution/editing tasks and deadlines.
    • Scope limitations.
  3. Revise the paper.
    • All authors incorporate feedback (in suggesting mode) and address dissent in shared google doc.
    • Lead author accepts/rejects revisions.
    • Lead author sends paper to the authors for review
  4. When all contributing authors have given their approval, the paper may be sent to Shannon for his editorial pass.
  5. When Shannon is finished, he sends the finalized paper back to lead author for acceptance.
    • By default, only the lead author is required for this step (this is because Shannon's edits will not substantively change the paper).
    • However, if the team would like, they may request additional members review. It's the lead author's responsibility to drive this (i.e. determine if they want to review and ensure they accept).
  6. Lead author (or additional team members, if applicable) accepts.
  7. Shannon posts the final version of the paper.

Healthy Management of Conflicts

Teams should make every effort to resolve conflicts within their group by following a process similar to the one stated below:

It's very important that this phase remain respectful and solution-oriented. If the conflict results in violations of the RWoT Code of Conduct, any person witnessing the violation should report the incident immediately to the Community Resiliency Team.

  1. Lead authors should make sure they have the agreement of all the team before they finalize the paper!!!
  2. Lead authors should try to address concerns of team members unless they conflict with the lead's own view of the paper.
  3. Group members should contact the Community Resiliency Team if these things aren't occurring. The CRT will remind the group leader of points (A) and (B) and will give voice to members who don't feel able to directly confront a group leader.
  4. If a group member doesn't feel like their disagreements have been addressed by a final paper, they can withdraw their name from the paper.
  5. If a group member doesn't feel like their disagreements have been addressed by a final paper, they can write a rebuttal to the paper (and request that it is included in the original paper).
  6. If a group member doesn't feel like their disagreements have been addressed by a final paper, they can write an alternate paper.

Any conflicts that are not be resolved through the above process should be reported to the Community Resiliency Team who will then review the conflict on a case by case basis.

This is version 1.0 of the RWoT paper best practices. This is a living document and will be updated as necessary by the RWoT Community Resiliency Team.
